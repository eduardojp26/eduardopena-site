Fallacy & Dark UX Key

  1. Ad Populum (Bandwagon) — "Over 3 million people" section: Uses the fact that a lot of people buy the product as the reason you should too, which isn't actually evidence it works.
  2. Appeal to Authority — "A doctor literally recommends it" section: Cites a PhD's endorsement as proof the product is effective, without providing any actual data or studies.
  3. Appeal to Nature — "All natural" section: Claims that because VitaMax Pro is natural, it's automatically safe — ignoring that plenty of natural things are harmful.
  4. False Dichotomy — "Two options" section: Frames the choice as either buying VitaMax Pro or feeling awful forever, leaving out every other option (sleep, diet, exercise, other supplements, etc.).
  5. Hasty Generalization — "People are noticing a difference" section: Surveys 5 customers, all of whom say they felt better, and uses that to claim it works for everyone.
  6. Slippery Slope — Hero/banner section: Implies that if you don't buy right now, you'll inevitably spiral into poor health and missing work, without any logical justification for that chain of events.

Dark UI/UX Element

Trick Question / Misleading Language — discount signup section: The checkbox is pre-checked by default, and the label uses a double negative: "Uncheck this box if you do not wish to NOT receive emails." This is taken directly from the type of example in the reading — the user genuinely can't tell whether leaving it checked means they're opting in or out. It exploits cognitive laziness so most people just leave it and end up subscribed without meaning to.
