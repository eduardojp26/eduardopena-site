Fallacy & Dark UX Key

  1. Slippery Slope — Hero section: Claims that not reaching out will inevitably lead to losing an opportunity to someone else, with no logical justification for each step in the chain.
  2. Appeal to Authority — About section: Uses Morgan Stanley's selection as proof Eduardo is exceptional, without providing actual performance data or metrics.
  3. Ad Populum (Bandwagon) — Experience section: Argues that because 300 people rely on his work, it must be good — popularity isn't evidence of quality.
  4. Appeal to Nature — Skills section: Claims "organic" experience is inherently more trustworthy than structured training, which isn't necessarily true.
  5. False Dichotomy — Options section: Presents only two choices (hire Eduardo or settle for less), ignoring every other qualified candidate.
  6. Hasty Generalization — Testimonials section: Uses two informal quotes to conclude that "everyone" considers him exceptional — a tiny, non-representative sample.
